01 · Identity

Who We Are

Before strategy, vision. Before vision, foundation.

"Pneuma Worship Collective creates Spirit-led, Word-rooted worship music that carries the sound of the Zoe Household Church to worshippers globally."

We are the worship music expression of Zoe Household Global, led by Pastor Dolapo Lawal. A collective, not a solo act — rooted in Lagos (Yaba and Ikeja), with church locations in Atlanta and Houston, and active presence in Abeokuta and London.

We produce original, live, Word-centred worship music. Every song is a theological statement. Every production decision serves encounter, not performance. We write from revelation — and we see what we sing.

What Makes Us Distinct

  • Theological depth woven into melody — not decoration, revelation
  • Live worship atmosphere that translates on record and on screen
  • Collective leadership — multiple voices, one sound
  • Song-birthing stories that build listener connection and adoption
  • Nigeria-rooted, globally accessible sound in the CCM tradition

Our Story

  • 2020Jubilee Choir begins intentional original songwriting. Vision shifts from congregational service to original worship output.
  • 2024Rebranded as Pneuma Worship Collective. Debut live album Pneuma (Live) released. Amen Hallelujah and Abba's Beloved drop as singles.
  • 2025Inhabit released. Gbagada studio conversion begins. Strategy team formalises with cross-continent membership.
  • 2026Gathering of Believers and Responsible Father released. First TOWDAH sells out. Opemipo and Pipe join. Album drops 10 July 2026.

02 · Our 12-Month Ambition

Building a Defining CCM Sound

Over the next 12 months, Pneuma will strengthen its Nigerian home base, deepen diaspora engagement, and establish a repeatable engine for excellent worship releases and community adoption.

I
Strengthen the Home Base
Every Zoe campus singing Pneuma. Every Sunday reinforcing adoption. TOWDAH as a quarterly cultural moment. The congregation as our most loyal amplifier and distribution network.
II
Deepen Diaspora Engagement
Atlanta, Houston, and London are not just locations — they are high-value streaming markets. Every Zoe branch abroad is an amplifier. Diaspora-targeted ads, Zoe ATL cross-promotion, and eventually TOWDAH nights on their soil.
III
Build the Release Engine
A consistent, documented, repeatable system for releasing worship content — video, audio, lyrics, song stories — that does not depend on any single person and does not stop between albums.

04 · What the Data Is Telling Us

Reading the Evidence

The numbers point clearly. The opportunity is real and the demand is proven.

  • →Organic demand is proven. TOWDAH sold out without paid promotion. Spotify listeners nearly doubled in one ad week. The audience exists — it is waiting to be reached.
  • →Live worship is the strongest content format. Full live sessions consistently outperform standalone singles in watch time and shares. This is the product to build around.
  • →Nigeria is the home base. Our primary community and adoption engine. Every branch, campus, and cell is an amplifier that has not yet been fully activated.
  • →Diaspora growth must be cultivated intentionally. Atlanta, Houston, and London are high-value streaming markets. Spotify Nigeria pays a fraction of US/UK per-stream rates. Diaspora viewership is a financial lever, not just a reach goal.
  • →Consistent releases and packaging are the biggest immediate opportunities. We have content. We have an audience. The gap is a reliable system for getting music out.
"The sound is strong. The system around it is still being built."
Platform Opportunity
Nigeria streaming rate vs. US/UK: 10–20× gap per stream
Viewership location matters more than channel location for revenue
Ad targeting set to US regions already driving diaspora discovery

05 · The Strategic Gaps

What We Are Solving

Four gaps stand between where we are and where we are going.

Gap 01
Visibility
The music is not yet reaching enough of the potential audience. 12K Instagram and 20K Spotify listeners are proof of concept — not scale. Lyrics discoverability, SEO, and sustained paid promotion are largely untapped.
Gap 02
Consistency
Releases, social content, and campaigns are not yet predictable. Release dates have not been specific or coordinated. Responsible Father dropped without team alignment. Publishing must become a system, not an event.
Gap 03
Production Quality Bar
No unified visual theme, unclear storytelling across campaigns, and insufficiently specific release timelines. Pipe joins as creative anchor — standardising Pneuma's brand story across all platforms and campuses.
Gap 04
Capacity & Infrastructure
Team, budget process, studio, finance structure, and role clarity are all still developing. The TOWDAH venue was nearly lost due to disbursement delays. These are structural risks that must be resolved before scale.
"The sound is strong. The system around it is still being built."

07 · Strategic Choices

How We Win

Strategy is as much about what we will not do as what we will. These are the team's explicit commitments.

We Will
  • →Build around live worship as our primary content format
  • →Release consistently rather than wait for perfect moments
  • →Grow Nigeria first while cultivating diaspora demand in parallel
  • →Invest in creative and production excellence as a strategic priority
  • →Use Zoe as a worship adoption and amplification engine
  • →Treat TOWDAH as a flagship content, culture, and community platform
  • →Stagger releases with teaser → full video cadence to build anticipation
  • →Target diaspora in US regions via ad settings to grow high-value streaming
We Will Not
  • ✗Pursue too many content formats at once without owning any of them
  • ✗Use paid ads to compensate for weak or unfinished content
  • ✗Produce one-off events without a documented release and follow-up plan
  • ✗Allow role ownership and content authority to remain unclear
  • ✗Release content without coordinated watch parties and team communication
  • ✗Continue with outstanding payments or unresolved finance structure
  • ✗Treat CCM as secondary to production — the song is always primary
  • ✗Mix strategy priorities with day-to-day admin in the same dashboard

12 · Leadership Asks

Leadership Asks

The decisions that unlock the strategy. Each item requires a clear answer.

Decision 01
Approve the Quarterly Budget Envelope
Confirm the 9-month ₦20M framework and establish a pre-approved quarterly pool for events and production that does not require individual line-item approval. The TOWDAH venue was nearly lost. This cannot happen again.
Decision 02
Confirm Finance Owner & Disbursement Process
Kojo to identify and appoint a dedicated finance resource for Pneuma. DistroKid must be moved from Tolu's personal card to a Pneuma account. Outstanding payments (Yanmife salary + David Richman TOWDAH fees) must be cleared.
Decision 03
Endorse the Album & Video Release Calendar
Approve the 10 July album drop and the full accompanying video release schedule. Confirm PMike's authority to execute the calendar in coordination with Opemipo and the creative team without per-release approval.
Decision 04
Authorise Church-Wide Adoption & Promotion
Formally authorise one Pneuma song every Sunday across all Zoe branches. Enable Zainab and campus media heads to actively push content. Activate Zoe ATL for diaspora cross-promotion. The congregation is our biggest untapped distribution network.
Decision 05
Confirm TOWDAH Frequency & Purpose
Formally confirm TOWDAH as a quarterly event (last Saturday of each quarter) and endorse scaling to 300+ capacity. Clarify TOWDAH's dual purpose: live worship recording and community building — not just a concert.
Decision 06
Approve Investment in Creative & Production Capability
Approve the Gbagada studio conversion and creative production spend. Approve the finance governance proposal (Kemi) covering how Zoe capitalises Pneuma, how revenue is shared, and how Pneuma funds are tracked and governed.
